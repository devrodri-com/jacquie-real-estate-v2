# Jacquie Real Estate V2 - Strategy

## 1. Product definition

Jacquie Real Estate V2 is a trust-led real estate website for Jacquie Zarate in Miami.

The site is not a property marketplace, a search portal, or a catalog-first realtor website. Its main job is to help international owners, buyers, and investors feel they have a trusted person in Miami who can guide them, respond directly, and help them make confident decisions.

The product being communicated is not inventory first. The product is confidence, clarity, personal guidance, and local follow-up.

The primary conversion path is direct conversation through WhatsApp.

## 2. Target audience

The primary audience is international owners, buyers, and investors who are interested in Miami, Sunny Isles, or South Florida and need trustworthy local guidance.

The most important user profiles are:

- Remote property owners who need help managing a short-term rental.
- Investors who want their property to generate income without managing daily operations themselves.
- First-time or remote buyers who need clarity before taking action.
- International clients who need someone local, responsive, and reliable.
- Buyers who may explore listings or pre-construction opportunities after trust has been established.

ES, EN, and FR-CA are all first-level languages for the MVP.

None of these languages should be treated as secondary, optional, incomplete, or added later. The core narrative must remain consistent across all three languages, with possible tone nuances by audience but no hierarchy of importance.

Language entry and detection should respect the user's route or saved preference when applicable. A francophone user should not feel that FR-CA is a partial translation or a later adaptation.

## 3. Main user problem

The user is not only looking for properties. The user is trying to reduce uncertainty.

Typical concerns include:

- Can I trust someone while I am outside the United States?
- Who will take care of the property if I am not there?
- Will guests be selected carefully?
- What happens if something breaks or needs maintenance?
- Will someone respond quickly and personally?
- Can the property generate income without becoming a daily burden?
- Where do I start if I want to invest in Miami?
- How do I avoid making a decision without understanding the process?

The site should answer these concerns before asking the user to explore inventory.

## 4. Main promise

The core promise is:

> Your property and your decisions in Miami can be handled with guidance, care, and direct follow-up from someone you can trust.

For Property Management, the promise is not only profitability. The stronger promise is:

> Your property can generate income without requiring you to manage the daily operation yourself.

The supporting ideas are:

- Tranquility.
- Property care.
- Occupancy support.
- Careful guest selection.
- Clear owner communication.
- Local presence.
- Real follow-up from Jacquie.

## 5. Positioning

Jacquie should be positioned as:

> Your trusted person in Miami.

More specifically, she is a combination of advisor, real estate professional / Realtor context, and reliable local contact.

She should not be positioned as a cold property manager, a generic agent, or a luxury concierge detached from the client. The tone should communicate: someone who accompanies you, answers you, explains the process, and takes care of what matters.

Strategic attributes:

- Personal.
- Direct.
- Trustworthy.
- Calm.
- Local.
- Multilingual site experience.
- Practical.
- Premium without feeling distant.

Credentials and trust signals that can be used:

- Florida real estate / Realtor license context, using the final formal wording Jacquie confirms.
- Miami Life Realty brokerage.
- Experience with international clients.
- Complete ES / EN / FR-CA website experience.
- Direct human communication confirmed in Spanish and English.
- Direct French communication should not be claimed unless confirmed.
- Miami / Sunny Isles / South Florida focus.
- Personalized attention.
- End-to-end follow-up.
- Real Property Management process explained by Jacquie.

Do not invent testimonials, measurable case studies, exact years of experience, or number of managed properties unless they are later confirmed.

Pending wording decision:

- Confirm whether Jacquie can formally use `Realtor®` in final interface copy.
- Until confirmed, do not make the registered term essential to the core message.

## 6. Service hierarchy

The business hierarchy is:

1. Property Management
2. Listings
3. Pre-construction

### Property Management

Property Management is the strategic center of the site.

The confirmed focus is short-term rentals / Airbnb-style rental management.

It may include:

- Publishing the property on platforms such as Airbnb.
- Using Jacquie's own contact network.
- Careful guest selection.
- Direct guest communication.
- Personalized check-in.
- Collection and transfer of payments to the owner.
- Coordination of repairs or maintenance with owner approval.

Do not assume long-term rental management unless it is explicitly defined later.

### Listings

Listings should function as tangible market evidence.

They help show that there are real opportunities and that Jacquie is connected to the market, but they must not become the center of the Home experience.

Listings should support the trust funnel. They should not turn the site into a marketplace.

### Pre-construction

Pre-construction should exist as a secondary opportunity path.

It should be framed as:

> If you also want to evaluate pre-construction opportunities, Jacquie can guide you.

It should not dominate the Home, the navigation, or the primary positioning.

## 7. Brand tone

The tone should be calm, direct, warm, and premium.

It should feel like a knowledgeable person explaining the path clearly, not like a sales campaign or real estate portal.

### Visual direction

V2 should use a deep plum / aubergine visual direction, not green, navy, brown, or a visual system inherited from Esteban/V1.

Approved V2 palette:

- `primary`: `#3B274A`
- `accent`: `#A98BB8`
- `surface`: `#F8F5FA`
- `foreground`: `#2B2530`
- `white`: `#FFFFFF`

The purple direction should feel premium, editorial, restrained, and sophisticated. It should not become bright, saturated, decorative, or overly feminine.

Use language that reinforces:

- Trust.
- Clarity.
- Care.
- Local support.
- Personal follow-up.
- Direct conversation.
- Reduced uncertainty.

Avoid:

- Aggressive sales language.
- Overpromising returns.
- Generic realtor claims.
- Technical investment jargon too early.
- Portal-style copy.
- Luxury cliches without substance.
- Claims that require proof the project does not yet have.

## 8. What Home must communicate in the first 10 seconds

Within the first 10 seconds, the user should understand:

1. Jacquie is a trusted local person in Miami.
2. The site is for owners, buyers, and investors who need guidance, not just inventory.
3. Property Management / short-term rental support is a core strength.
4. The user can start with a simple direct conversation.
5. The experience is personal, calm, and reliable.

The Home should establish trust before exploration.

Recommended narrative order:

1. Trust and direct positioning.
2. Jacquie as the person behind the service.
3. Property Management as the dominant business priority.
4. How the support works.
5. WhatsApp conversion.
6. Listings and Pre-construction as secondary credibility paths.

Primary Home CTA:

> Hablar con Jacquie

Contextual CTAs may include:

- Consultar por mi propiedad
- Quiero invertir en Miami

But the dominant Home action should remain simple and human.

## 9. What V2 must not do

V2 must not:

- Become a prettier version of V1.
- Reuse Esteban's conceptual structure.
- Start from listings, projects, filters, or investment metrics.
- Treat Property Management, Listings, and Pre-construction as equal priorities.
- Make the Home feel like a portal or marketplace.
- Push users into exploration before building trust.
- Hide Jacquie until late in the page.
- Let Pre-construction dominate the narrative.
- Let Listings compete with the main conversion path.
- Invent proof that does not exist.
- Use generic luxury language instead of specific trust-building language.
- Let implementation tools decide product hierarchy.
- Build architecture before strategy, narrative, and hierarchy are clear.

## 10. Decision criteria for future sections

Any future section should be evaluated with these questions:

- Does this increase trust?
- Does this reduce uncertainty for a remote or international client?
- Does this make Jacquie feel more personal, credible, and reachable?
- Does this support the main WhatsApp conversion path?
- Does this reinforce Property Management as the strategic priority?
- Does this keep Listings as support rather than the main product?
- Does this keep Pre-construction in a secondary role?
- Does this feel premium, calm, and specific to Jacquie?
- Does this help ES, EN, and FR-CA users understand what to do next without making any language feel secondary?
- Is the claim supported by real proof or confirmed business facts?

If a section does not clearly support trust, guidance, Property Management, or direct conversation, it should be removed, delayed, or reframed.
