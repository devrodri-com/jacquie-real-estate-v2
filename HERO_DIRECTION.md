# Hero Direction

## Status

Strategic direction for the first real V2 visual block: Header + Hero.

No implementation yet.

## 1. Hero Objective

The Hero must make the user understand the offer in the first 5-8 seconds:

- Jacquie is the trusted local person in Miami.
- The site is for people who need clarity, guidance, and follow-up, not just property browsing.
- Property Management is a primary commercial strength.
- The next step is a direct conversation, preferably through WhatsApp.

The Hero should reduce uncertainty before it introduces inventory. It should feel like a confident personal introduction, not a portal entry page.

## 2. Dominant Message

Dominant idea:

> You do not have to manage Miami real estate decisions from a distance alone. Jacquie is your trusted person on the ground.

The Hero should connect three ideas without overloading the fold:

- Personal trust.
- Miami real estate guidance.
- Property ownership / short-term rental management support.

Listings and Pre-construction can exist later as proof and options, but they should not define the first impression.

## 5. CTA Hierarchy

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Start a direct conversation with Jacquie.

Recommended labels:

- ES: Hablar con Jacquie
- EN: Talk to Jacquie
- FR-CA: Contacter Jacquie

Destination:

- WhatsApp should be the default primary CTA destination.

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Learn about the highest-priority service before committing to conversation.

Recommended labels:

- ES: Ver gestión de propiedades
- EN: View Property Management
- FR-CA: Voir la gestion de propriétés

Destination:

- `/[locale]/property-management`

### When to use WhatsApp

Use WhatsApp for:

- General Hero CTA.
- Property owner inquiry.
- Remote owner/investor uncertainty.
- Any message framed around "talk", "consult", "ask", or "start".

### When to use contact

Use contact as a secondary path when:

- The user prefers a form.
- The section is lower on the page.
- The CTA is less urgent or more formal.

Contact should not replace WhatsApp as the main Hero conversion path.

### What must not be the primary Hero CTA

Do not use these as primary CTA:

- Browse Listings.
- View Projects.
- Search Properties.
- Explore Pre-construction.
- See Investment Opportunities.
- Learn More.

Those actions either make inventory dominant or dilute the trust-led conversion.

## 6. What Must Not Appear In The Hero

The Hero must not include:

- Listings grid.
- Property cards.
- Search bars.
- Filters.
- Service grid.
- Metrics about ROI, returns, occupancy, volume, years, rankings, or testimonials unless verified.
- Pre-construction as the main hook.
- Multiple equal CTAs.
- Too many navigation choices.
- Generic luxury claims.
- Any copy that implies Jacquie personally communicates in French.

The Hero should not feel like a marketplace, a catalog, or a generic brokerage landing page.

## 7. Visual Direction

The Hero should feel:

- Premium.
- Editorial.
- Human.
- Calm.
- Direct.
- Trustworthy.
- Personal to Jacquie.

Visual system:

- Use the approved deep plum / aubergine palette:
  - `primary`: `#3B274A`
  - `accent`: `#A98BB8`
  - `surface`: `#F8F5FA`
  - `foreground`: `#2B2530`
  - `white`: `#FFFFFF`
- Use purple as depth and identity, not decoration.
- Keep the fold quiet and intentional.
- Prefer large typography, measured spacing, and one strong visual focal point.
- Avoid heavy cards, nested cards, glossy portal effects, or bright gradients.

The Hero should look like a trust-led advisory brand, not an inventory platform.

## 8. Image / Background Direction

### Preferred image strategy

The ideal Hero image should include Jacquie or strongly imply her personal presence.

Best direction:

- A refined portrait or environmental photo of Jacquie.
- Calm Miami context.
- Natural confidence, not heavy glamour.
- Space for text and responsive cropping.

If using a photo of Jacquie, it should make the first impression more human and more ownable. This supports the strategic need for Jacquie to appear early.

### If current photos are not enough

If current photos feel too retouched, too cropped, or too inherited from V1, the better direction is:

- Commission or select a new portrait of Jacquie.
- Shoot in a calm interior, balcony, lobby, or soft Miami environment.
- Avoid obvious luxury staging.
- Avoid real estate agent stock-photo poses.
- Keep clothing and background aligned with deep plum / warm neutral styling.

### Alternative background strategy

If Jacquie portrait assets are not approved for the Hero, use Miami imagery as a supporting background, but pair it with an early Jacquie introduction close below the fold.

Miami imagery should:

- Feel calm and atmospheric.
- Avoid loud sunset/orange dominance where possible.
- Avoid making the site feel like a destination marketing page.
- Support the idea of local presence, not replace Jacquie.

### What to avoid

Avoid:

- Pre-construction renderings as Hero image.
- Project-specific imagery above the fold.
- Listing interiors as Hero image.
- Search/catalog screenshots.
- Generic skyline images that could belong to any realtor site.
- Dark, glossy, luxury-brokerage styling.
- Busy city images that compete with the headline.

## 10. Header Direction

The initial Header should be minimal and conversion-aware.

Recommended structure:

- Left: Jacquie Zarate wordmark/name.
- Pending brand variant: Jacquie Zarate Realtor, only if final formal wording is confirmed.
- Center or right: a very small set of links.
- Right: language selector ES / EN / FR-CA.
- Optional primary CTA on desktop: Hablar con Jacquie / Talk to Jacquie / Contacter Jacquie.

Do not use "Miami Real Estate" as a brand qualifier in the Header. It is too generic for V2 and risks pulling the experience back toward a standard brokerage or portal identity.

Recommended initial links:

- Property Management.
- About.
- Contact.

Do not include Listings or Pre-construction in the first Header iteration unless there is a later strategic decision to expose them. They can appear later in secondary navigation or lower-page support.

### Header behavior

- Keep it quiet and readable.
- Avoid bulky nav bars.
- Avoid portal-style menus.
- Avoid search.
- Avoid "Properties" as the dominant nav item.
- Avoid many equal service links.
- Keep language switching visible but not dominant.

### Mobile header

Mobile should prioritize:

- Brand/name.
- Language access.
- One clear menu or contact action.

Do not crowd mobile header with multiple service paths.

## 11. Implementation Risks

When Cursor implements Header + Hero later, it must control these risks:

- Recreating V1's portal/catalog logic.
- Adding Listings or Pre-construction above the fold.
- Making the Hero a service menu.
- Using too many CTAs.
- Making Property Management invisible or too secondary.
- Making the Header look like a real estate marketplace.
- Using project/precon imagery because it is visually dramatic.
- Using generic Miami skyline imagery without enough Jacquie/personality.
- Overusing purple as decoration instead of identity.
- Creating a page that feels like "V1 but prettier" instead of a new trust-led V2.
- Writing FR-CA copy that implies direct French-speaking human support.

The implementation should protect one idea:

> Jacquie is the trusted local person who helps you make and manage Miami real estate decisions with clarity.
