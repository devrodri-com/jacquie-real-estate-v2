# Jacquie Real Estate V2 - Home Architecture

## Purpose

This document translates the approved strategy into the narrative structure of the Home page.

It does not define components, layouts, code, or implementation details. Its job is to protect hierarchy, conversion logic, and product clarity before UI decisions are made.

The Home must behave as:

> A trust-led landing page with secondary exploration.

It must not behave as:

- A real estate portal.
- A listings marketplace.
- A pre-construction catalog.
- A neutral navigation hub.
- A prettier version of V1.

## Core Home objective

The Home should make the user feel:

> I have found a trusted person in Miami who can guide me, provide a direct contact path, and help me manage or evaluate a real estate decision with less uncertainty.

The main conversion is direct conversation through WhatsApp.

The Home should not try to close a transaction. It should earn enough trust for the user to start a conversation.

## Recommended order

1. Hero: Trust and direct positioning
2. Early Jacquie section: The person behind the guidance
3. Property Management: Strategic service focus
4. How the support works: Reduce operational uncertainty
5. Primary conversion moment: Talk to Jacquie
6. Listings as market evidence
7. Pre-construction as future secondary opportunity path
8. Final conversion: Simple WhatsApp close, pending brief

This order should not be treated as decorative. It is the conversion logic of the page.

Trust Proof decision:

- Do not create a separate Trust Proof section on Home.
- Trust should be concentrated in the early Jacquie section and reinforced naturally in Property Management.
- Confirmed credentials can appear inside those sections, but should not become a standalone proof block.

## 1. Hero: Trust and direct positioning

### Objective

Establish in the first seconds that this is a personal, trust-based Miami real estate experience, not an inventory-first website.

### What it communicates

- Jacquie is a trusted local person in Miami.
- The user can get guidance before making a decision.
- The site is relevant to owners, buyers, and investors.
- Property Management / short-term rental support is a central strength.
- The next step can be direct WhatsApp contact, not a form-heavy process.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Start contact with Jacquie through WhatsApp.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Hablar con Jacquie
- EN: Talk to Jacquie
- FR-CA: Contacter Jacquie

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Learn how Jacquie can help with a property in Miami.

This should stay subordinate to the WhatsApp action.

### What must not happen here

- No search bar.
- No property filters.
- No listing grid.
- No project carousel.
- No investment metrics.
- No pre-construction-first messaging.
- No generic luxury slogan detached from Jacquie.

## 2. Early Jacquie section: The person behind the guidance

### Objective

Make Jacquie visible early because trust depends on the person, not only the service.

### What it communicates

- Jacquie is the local point of contact.
- She gives personalized attention.
- She supports international clients.
- The website supports ES / EN / FR-CA as first-level languages.
- Direct human communication is confirmed in Spanish and English.
- French (Canada) is part of the site experience, but direct French communication should not be claimed unless confirmed.
- Her role combines advisor, real estate professional / Realtor context, and trusted local presence.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Start contact with Jacquie.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Hablar con Jacquie
- EN: Talk to Jacquie
- FR-CA: Contacter Jacquie

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Understand who Jacquie is and why she can help.

This may lead to a deeper About path, but it should not pull attention away from the WhatsApp conversion.

## 3. Property Management: Strategic service focus

### Objective

Make Property Management the dominant business priority of the Home.

### What it communicates

- Jacquie helps owners generate income from short-term rentals without having to manage the daily operation themselves.
- The service is focused on care, guest selection, communication, occupancy support, and owner peace of mind.
- The offer is practical and specific, not abstract.

### Confirmed scope to communicate

- Airbnb-style short-term rental support.
- Publishing on platforms such as Airbnb.
- Use of Jacquie's contact network.
- Careful guest selection.
- Direct guest communication.
- Personalized check-in.
- Collection and transfer of payments to the owner.
- Repairs or maintenance coordinated with owner approval.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Ask about my property.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Consultar por mi propiedad
- EN: Ask about my property
- FR-CA: Demander pour ma propriete

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Understand what the management support includes.

This should explain the process, not create a separate sales funnel above WhatsApp.

### What must not happen here

- Do not overpromise returns.
- Do not imply long-term rental management unless later confirmed.
- Do not make Property Management just one card among equal services.
- Do not bury the operational details that create trust.

## 4. How the support works: Reduce operational uncertainty

### Objective

Answer the user's practical fears before they need to ask.

### What it communicates

- The owner does not need to manage every detail alone.
- Jacquie provides follow-up from setup through guest coordination and owner communication.
- Maintenance and repairs are coordinated with approval, not improvised.
- The process is understandable even for someone outside Miami.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Ask how this would work for my property.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Consultar mi caso
- EN: Discuss my situation
- FR-CA: Presenter ma situation

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Continue reading the process.

This section should reduce friction, not introduce complex service tiers.

## 5. Trust reinforcement decision: no standalone section

### Decision

Do not create a separate Trust Proof section on Home.

### Where trust proof belongs

- JacquieTrustSection should carry the main personal credibility signals.
- Property Management should reinforce trust through clear operational scope and owner reassurance.
- About can hold deeper biography and credentials.

### Allowed proof points

- Florida real estate / Realtor license context, with final formal wording confirmed before launch.
- Miami Life Realty.
- Experience with international clients.
- Miami / Sunny Isles / South Florida focus.
- Complete ES / EN / FR-CA website experience.
- Direct human communication confirmed in Spanish and English.
- No direct French communication claim unless confirmed.

Do not invent testimonials, years, rankings, transaction volume, case studies, or number of managed properties.

## 6. Primary conversion moment: Talk to Jacquie

### Objective

Create a clear midpoint conversion opportunity after trust and Property Management have been established.

### What it communicates

- The user does not need to have everything figured out.
- The next step is a conversation.
- The first step can be a direct property-specific conversation.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Start WhatsApp conversation.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Hablar con Jacquie
- EN: Talk to Jacquie
- FR-CA: Contacter Jacquie

### Secondary CTA

Do not include a secondary CTA in this section.

Do not add context helper buttons such as:

- Ask about my property.
- I want to invest in Miami.
- I want to evaluate an opportunity.

Reason:

- This section exists to close the first journey with one clear WhatsApp action.
- Additional helper buttons weaken the conversion moment and reintroduce decision friction.

## 7. Listings as market evidence

### Objective

Use Listings to show tangible market presence without turning the Home into a marketplace.

### What it communicates

- There are real opportunities in the market.
- Jacquie can help users evaluate options.
- Listings are part of the support ecosystem, not the main product.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Talk about what kind of property makes sense.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Quiero invertir en Miami
- EN: I want to invest in Miami
- FR-CA: Je veux investir a Miami

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> View selected listings.

The listing path should be clearly secondary to conversation and guidance.

### How to keep Listings from competing

- Do not place Listings above Property Management.
- Do not use a full search experience on Home.
- Do not show too many items.
- Do not present Listings as the primary way to start.
- Frame them as selected examples or market evidence.
- Pair exploration with guidance language.

## 8. Pre-construction as future secondary opportunity path

### Objective

Acknowledge pre-construction as an available advisory path without letting it inherit the strategic weight of V1 or Esteban's structure.

Implementation status:

- Do not implement Precon yet.
- Before any implementation, create and approve `PRECON_SUPPORT_SECTION_BRIEF.md`.
- That brief must explicitly protect against returning to Esteban / preconstruction-first logic.

### What it communicates

- Jacquie can help users evaluate pre-construction opportunities if relevant.
- Pre-construction is one possible path, not the core identity of the site.
- The user should still start from guidance, not a project catalog.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Ask whether pre-construction is right for me.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Evaluar preconstruccion
- EN: Evaluate pre-construction
- FR-CA: Evaluer la preconstruction

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> View selected opportunities.

This should remain below the guidance CTA.

### How to keep Pre-construction from competing

- Do not place it before Jacquie or Property Management.
- Do not use project-first navigation on Home.
- Do not lead with financing plans, metrics, or developer-style claims.
- Do not make it visually louder than Property Management.
- Position it as an advisory extension.

Listings + Precon grouping:

- Do not decide yet whether Listings and Precon should be fused.
- A future combined support area such as "Selected paths" / "market references" may be considered after the Precon brief exists.
- Do not apply that grouping before Precon is strategically defined.

## 9. Final conversion: Simple WhatsApp close

### Objective

End the Home with a simple human next step.

Implementation status:

- Final CTA is not implemented yet.
- Before implementation, create and approve `FINAL_CTA_BRIEF.md`.

### What it communicates

- The user can start even if they are not sure what they need yet.
- Jacquie can guide them toward the right path.
- WhatsApp is the expected conversion, not a last-resort contact option.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Talk to Jacquie.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Hablar con Jacquie
- EN: Talk to Jacquie
- FR-CA: Contacter Jacquie

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Use a contact form if preferred.

The contact form is secondary.

## What gets eliminated from V1

The Home should eliminate:

- Portal-style structure.
- Equal-priority service blocks.
- Early Listings dominance.
- Early Pre-construction dominance.
- Project-first narrative.
- Search/filter behavior above the trust narrative.
- Excessive exploration before explaining Jacquie.
- Generic realtor copy.
- Investment metrics before trust.
- Any structure inherited from Esteban's pre-construction-first logic.

## What can be reused conceptually

The Home can preserve these ideas if reframed:

- Listings as tangible market evidence.
- Pre-construction as an available advisory path.
- Property Management content, but elevated to the primary service story.
- About/Jacquie content, but moved earlier and made more central.
- WhatsApp as the main conversion behavior.
- Miami / Sunny Isles / South Florida geographic focus.
- Existing confirmed trust signals.
- Calm, premium visual direction and the approved deep plum / aubergine V2 palette.

Conceptual reuse must not preserve V1's hierarchy.

## What must not appear above the fold

Above the fold must not include:

- Listing grids.
- Project cards.
- Search bars.
- Filters.
- Financing tables.
- Pre-construction catalog language.
- Multiple equal CTAs for unrelated paths.
- Generic "browse properties" as the dominant action.
- Claims about testimonials, results, years, rankings, or volume unless confirmed.
- Any message that makes inventory feel more important than Jacquie.

Above the fold should prioritize:

- Jacquie's trust positioning.
- Miami local guidance.
- Property ownership / investment uncertainty.
- Direct WhatsApp conversation.

## Listings and Pre-construction support rules

Listings and Pre-construction are allowed on the Home only if they support the trust funnel.

Current implementation status:

- Listings support can exist as a light lower Home section.
- Pre-construction is not approved for implementation yet and requires `PRECON_SUPPORT_SECTION_BRIEF.md`.
- Do not fuse Listings and Precon before the Precon brief defines the correct role.

They should:

- Appear after Jacquie, Property Management, and the main conversion logic.
- Be framed as selected paths, not the main product.
- Use guidance-oriented copy.
- Include conversation CTAs near exploration CTAs.
- Avoid search-heavy or catalog-heavy behavior on Home.
- Stay visually and narratively quieter than Property Management.

They should not:

- Become the reason the Home exists.
- Compete with WhatsApp as the main action.
- Create a marketplace feeling.
- Reintroduce Esteban's project-first structure.

## ES / EN / FR-CA experience rules

ES, EN, and FR-CA are first-level MVP languages.

The Home must work as a complete experience in all three languages.

Rules:

- The narrative order must remain consistent across ES, EN, and FR-CA.
- No language should receive fewer sections, weaker CTAs, missing content, or placeholder copy.
- Tone can adapt to audience nuance, but hierarchy cannot change.
- Route and saved language preference should be respected where applicable.
- CTAs should keep the same intent across languages even if wording changes naturally.
- FR-CA CTAs may invite the user to contact Jacquie, but must not imply confirmed French-language human support.
- FR-CA must feel written for a francophone user, not like a partial or late translation.
- Language switching should not reset the user's sense of place in the experience when avoidable.

The strategic message in every language is the same:

> Jacquie is the trusted local person in Miami who can guide you, help you manage your property, and support smart real estate decisions through direct conversation.

Direct human communication is confirmed in Spanish and English. FR-CA is a first-level site experience, but French-language human support should not be promised unless confirmed.

## Home decision checklist

Before approving any Home section, ask:

- Does this build trust before exploration?
- Does this make Jacquie visible and credible?
- Does this reinforce Property Management as the priority?
- Does this reduce uncertainty for a remote or international user?
- Does this keep WhatsApp as the primary conversion path?
- Does this keep Listings and Pre-construction secondary?
- Does this avoid unconfirmed claims?
- Does this work fully in ES, EN, and FR-CA?

If the answer is no, the section should be removed, moved lower, or reframed.
