# Process Section Brief

## Status

Pre-implementation brief.

No code yet. No Cursor prompt yet.

This section appears after the Home Property Management section.

## 1. Section Objective

The Process section should make Property Management feel simple, personal, and understandable.

Its job is to reduce uncertainty for an owner or investor who is considering leaving a Miami property in Jacquie's hands.

The user should understand:

- there is a clear first step;
- Jacquie evaluates the property and the owner's goals;
- management is coordinated with communication and owner approval where needed;
- the service is not a faceless operation.

This section should organize the process mentally without turning it into a corporate workflow.

It should answer:

> What happens after I reach out?

## 2. Recommended 3 Steps

Use exactly 3 steps.

### Step 1

Intent:

> Start with a direct conversation about the property.

What it covers:

- owner situation;
- property type;
- location;
- goals;
- current concerns.

### Step 2

Intent:

> Define the rental and management approach.

What it covers:

- short-term rental fit;
- publication needs;
- guest handling;
- check-in expectations;
- owner approval points.

### Step 3

Intent:

> Jacquie coordinates the daily details and keeps the owner informed.

What it covers:

- guest attention;
- stay follow-up;
- maintenance coordination;
- owner updates;
- payment/transfer coordination if applicable.

Do not add more steps on the Home. Deeper process details belong on `/property-management`.

## 4. CTA Recommendation

Use one CTA only.

Purpose:

> Continue the Property Management conversation through WhatsApp.

Labels:

- ES: `Consultar por mi propiedad`
- EN: `Ask about my property`
- FR-CA: `Demander de l'information sur ma propriété`

Destination:

- WhatsApp using the existing helper pattern.

CTA visual weight:

- Clear but quieter than the Property Management section CTA.
- This is a reinforcement CTA, not a new conversion hierarchy.

No secondary CTA is required in this section.

## 5. What Not To Include

Do not include:

- More than 3 steps.
- Detailed operational checklist.
- Full onboarding flow.
- Pricing strategy.
- Calendar management details.
- Cleaning/turnover details unless later confirmed.
- Payment transfer details unless explained carefully on the dedicated page.
- ROI claims.
- Guaranteed occupancy.
- Guaranteed profitability.
- Revenue projections.
- Listings.
- Precon.
- Search or filters.
- Project/property cards.
- Corporate workflow language.
- SaaS-style funnel language.
- Claims that Jacquie speaks French.

Avoid terms like:

- "Automated workflow."
- "Pipeline."
- "Scale your portfolio."
- "Maximize returns."
- "Guaranteed booking performance."

## 6. Visual Direction

The section should feel like a calm explanation, not a dashboard or process diagram.

Recommended structure:

1. Short section header.
2. Brief intro.
3. Three editorial steps.
4. One CTA.

Visual style:

- Light surface background.
- Fine dividers or understated numbering.
- No heavy cards.
- No icons unless extremely restrained.
- No arrows, flowchart, or SaaS-like progression.
- No big image required.

Placement:

- It should follow the Property Management section.
- It should visually lower the intensity after the dark plum service block.

The section should feel reassuring and easy to scan.

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

The section is acceptable if:

- It appears after the Home Property Management section.
- It uses exactly 3 steps.
- It explains the process in simple, human language.
- It is clearly for owners/investors considering short-term rental management.
- It does not duplicate the full `/property-management` page.
- It does not use corporate, SaaS, or dashboard-like language.
- It includes only one CTA to WhatsApp.
- It does not mention Listings or Precon.
- It does not promise ROI, occupancy, profitability, or metrics.
- It does not claim direct French-speaking support.
- ES / EN / FR-CA copy is complete and natural.
- FR-CA uses correct accents.
- The visual weight is calmer than the Property Management section.
