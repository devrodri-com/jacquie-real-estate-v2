# Property Management Page Brief

## Status

Pre-implementation brief.

No code yet. No Cursor prompt yet.

This document defines the first real version of the `/[locale]/property-management` page for V2.

## 1. Page Objective

The Property Management page must explain Jacquie's short-term rental management service with more clarity than the Home, while staying concise, human, and trust-led.

The page should help a property owner or investor understand:

- what Jacquie can help manage;
- who the service is for;
- how the first steps work;
- what remains under owner approval;
- why the next step is a direct conversation.

The goal is not to sell a guaranteed financial result.

The goal is to reduce uncertainty and make it easy for the owner to ask about their property.

## 2. Difference From The Home Block

### Home Property Management block

The Home block introduces the service.

It should:

- position Property Management as the main commercial priority;
- explain the basic value in a few seconds;
- show a maximum of 4 key responsibilities;
- keep the user moving through the Home narrative.

### Dedicated Property Management page

The dedicated page explains the service.

It should:

- expand the short-term rental management scope;
- clarify what Jacquie can coordinate;
- explain how owner approval works;
- answer the most important doubts without becoming a FAQ page;
- create a property-specific WhatsApp conversation.

The dedicated page should not duplicate the Home section word for word.

## 3. Approved Page Structure

First implementation:

1. Hero specific to Property Management.
2. For whom it is.
3. What I can manage.
4. How it works.
5. Transparency and approvals.
6. Final CTA.

FAQ is intentionally excluded from this version.

## 5. CTA Hierarchy

### Primary CTA

Primary CTA must open WhatsApp using the existing helper pattern.

Intent:

> Start a property-specific conversation.

Labels:

- ES: `Consultar por mi propiedad`
- EN: `Ask about my property`
- FR-CA: `Demander de l’information sur ma propriété`

### Secondary CTA

Secondary CTA may point to Contact because this is a deeper internal page and some users may prefer a non-WhatsApp path.

Labels:

- ES: `Contacto`
- EN: `Contact`
- FR-CA: `Contact`

Destination:

```text
/{locale}/contact
```

The Contact CTA must be visually quieter than WhatsApp.

Do not add extra helper CTAs such as:

- `Quiero invertir`
- `Evaluar oportunidad`
- `Ver listings`
- `Ver preconstrucción`

## 6. Content Included Now

Include in the first implementation:

- page-specific hero;
- who the service is for;
- service scope;
- simple process;
- transparency and approvals;
- final conversion block.

Use only verified service facts:

- short-term rental / Airbnb-style focus;

- own contact network when relevant;
- careful guest selection;
- guest attention;
- personalized check-in;
- payment collection and transfer to owner;
- repairs and maintenance coordinated with owner approval.

## 7. Content Reserved For Later

Do not include in the first implementation:

- FAQ section;
- pricing model;
- specific commission structure;
- number of properties managed;
- testimonials;
- case studies;
- owner portal;
- downloadable guide;
- detailed legal/tax explanation;
- long-term rental management;
- Listings or Pre-construction references.

FAQ may be added later after Jacquie confirms recurring questions and preferred answers.

## 8. What Not To Include

Do not include:

- ROI;
- guaranteed occupancy;
- guaranteed profitability;
- income promises;
- performance metrics;
- fake testimonials;
- years of experience unless confirmed;
- number of properties managed unless confirmed;
- Pre-construction;
- Listings;
- search, filters, maps, or property cards;
- language suggesting Jacquie handles everything without owner approval;
- claims of direct human communication in French;
- V1 components, V1 navigation patterns, or V1 catalog logic.

Avoid phrases like:

- `full-service platform`;
- `maximize returns`;
- `guaranteed income`;
- `hands-off investment`;
- `we take care of everything`;
- `best ROI`;
- `exclusive opportunity`.

## 9. FR-CA Rules

FR-CA is a first-level website experience and must be complete.

Rules:

- Use proper French with accents.
- Public route remains `/fr-ca`.
- Internal content locale remains `fr-CA`.
- Do not expose `/fr`.
- Do not claim Jacquie personally speaks French.
- Avoid CTA language that implies a confirmed live French conversation.
- Use request-oriented CTAs in FR-CA, such as `Demander de l’information sur ma propriété`.

Human communication is confirmed in Spanish and English only.

## 10. Implementation Risks

Main risks:

- Repeating the Home Property Management block instead of expanding it.
- Making the page too long too early.
- Sounding corporate, operational, or agency-like.
- Overpromising results around occupancy, profitability, or income.
- Creating too many CTAs and weakening WhatsApp as the main action.
- Making FR-CA feel like a partial translation.
- Suggesting direct French-speaking support.
- Pulling in V1 service sections or catalog patterns.
- Turning this into a generic property management page instead of Jacquie's personal advisory service.

## 11. Acceptance Criteria

The first implementation is acceptable if:

- the page replaces the placeholder with real, complete content;
- ES, EN, and FR-CA have equivalent content quality;
- the page uses first-person voice;
- WhatsApp is the primary conversion action;
- Contact is secondary and visually quieter;
- the page explains short-term rental management clearly;
- owner approval is explicit;
- no ROI, occupancy, profitability, metrics, or guarantees appear;
- no Listings or Pre-construction appear;
- no direct human French communication is promised;
- the page feels premium, calm, and personal;
- it does not copy V1 components or V1 page structure;
- lint and build pass after implementation.
