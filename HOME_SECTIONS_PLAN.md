# Jacquie Real Estate V2 - Home Sections Plan

## Purpose

This document turns the approved Home architecture into an operational section plan.

It does not define components, layout implementation, code, or Cursor prompts. Its purpose is to make the Home clear enough that implementation can later follow narrative, hierarchy, and conversion without inventing product decisions.

The Home must remain:

> Trust first, Jacquie early, Property Management dominant, Listings and Pre-construction supportive, WhatsApp as the main conversion path.

## Section order

1. Hero: Trusted person in Miami
2. Jacquie: Personal trust and local guidance
3. Property Management: Short-term rental support
4. Management process: How the support works
5. Mid-page conversion: Start the conversation
6. Listings: Selected market evidence
7. Pre-construction: Future secondary advisory path, pending brief
8. Final CTA: Talk to Jacquie, pending brief

This order should be protected unless a future strategic decision explicitly changes the Home narrative.

Closed decision:

- Do not create a separate Trust Proof section on Home.
- Trust proof lives inside JacquieTrustSection and is reinforced naturally in Property Management.
- Process remains independent for now because its first version was visually validated.
- Process can be compacted in a future iteration if the Home feels too long.

## 1. Hero: Trusted person in Miami

### Objective

Make the user understand immediately that this site is about trust, guidance, and direct contact with Jacquie in Miami, not about browsing inventory.

### Main content

The hero should communicate:

- Jacquie as a trusted local person in Miami.
- Guidance for owners, buyers, and investors.
- Confidence for international or remote users.
- Property Management / short-term rental support as an important strength.
- WhatsApp as the simplest next step.

The message should feel human and specific. It should not sound like a generic realtor headline.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Start contact with Jacquie through WhatsApp.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Hablar con Jacquie
- EN: Talk to Jacquie
- FR-CA: Contacter Jacquie

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Understand how Jacquie can help with a property in Miami.

The secondary CTA must be visually quieter than the WhatsApp CTA.

### Conceptually reused from V1

- WhatsApp as the main conversion behavior.
- Miami / Sunny Isles / South Florida relevance.
- Jacquie as the personal brand.

### Eliminated from V1

- Inventory-first hero.
- Search or filters above the fold.
- Project-first or pre-construction-first language.
- Multiple equal CTAs that split attention.

### Copy to avoid

- "Find your dream home" as the main message.
- "Browse properties" as the dominant action.
- "Best investment opportunities" without first building trust.
- Any return, ranking, testimonial, or volume claim that is not confirmed.

### Visual hierarchy rules

- Jacquie/trust message must be the dominant signal.
- WhatsApp CTA must be the clearest action.
- Any secondary path must feel optional.
- No listing cards, project cards, search bars, filters, or financing tables above the fold.
- The fold should create confidence, not exploration pressure.

### Language rules

- ES / EN / FR-CA must all have complete hero copy.
- FR-CA CTA may invite contact, but must not imply confirmed French-speaking human support.
- Direct human communication is confirmed in Spanish and English only.

## 3. Property Management: Short-term rental support

### Objective

Make Property Management the dominant service story of the Home.

### Main content

This section should explain that Jacquie helps owners manage short-term rental properties so the property can generate income without the owner having to manage daily operations.

Confirmed scope:

- Airbnb-style short-term rental support.
- Publishing on platforms such as Airbnb.
- Use of Jacquie's contact network.
- Careful guest selection.
- Guest attention and coordination.
- Personalized check-in.
- Payment collection and transfer to the owner.
- Repairs or maintenance coordinated with owner approval.

The section should make the value practical:

> Less daily burden, more care, clearer follow-up, and a local person watching the operation.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Ask about my property.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Consultar por mi propiedad
- EN: Ask about my property
- FR-CA: Demander pour ma propriete

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Understand what the management support includes.

The secondary path should explain scope, not create a separate competing funnel.

### Conceptually reused from V1

- Existing Property Management content if factual and specific.
- Any prior explanation of included services, if aligned with confirmed scope.
- WhatsApp contact behavior from management-related CTAs.

### Eliminated from V1

- Property Management as one equal service among several.
- Generic service-card treatment.
- Weak placement below inventory exploration.
- Any language that focuses only on profitability.

### Copy to avoid

- "Guaranteed income."
- "Passive income with no effort."
- "We maximize returns" unless backed by proof and approved.
- Long-term rental management claims unless confirmed.
- Overly technical operations copy that makes the offer feel cold.

### Visual hierarchy rules

- This should be one of the strongest sections on the Home.
- It must feel more important than Listings and Pre-construction.
- The operational details should be visible enough to create trust.
- The section should not be reduced to a shallow three-card list.
- CTA should be specific to owners, but not exclude investors.

### Language rules

- ES / EN / FR-CA must explain the same confirmed service scope.
- FR-CA must not imply French-speaking owner support unless confirmed.
- CTAs can invite the user to submit or start contact about a property.

## 4. Management process: How the support works

Reduce operational uncertainty by showing that the service has a clear, understandable flow.

### Main content

- Who handles guest communication?
- How are guests selected?
- Who coordinates check-in?
- What happens if something needs repair?
- How does the owner stay informed?
- How are payments handled?

The process should feel calm and manageable, especially for remote owners.

### Primary CTA

Intent:

> Ask how this would work for my property.

Suggested labels:

- ES: Consultar mi caso
- EN: Discuss my situation
- FR-CA: Presenter ma situation

### Secondary CTA

Intent:

> Continue to trust proof or learn more about the process.

This section should reduce friction, not introduce complex packages.

### Conceptually reused from V1

- Any "how it works" content that explains a real process.
- Existing operational explanations that are factual.

### Eliminated from V1

- Process steps that are too generic.
- Any flow that treats all services equally.
- Long process copy that delays conversion.

### Copy to avoid

- "We take care of everything" if it hides owner approval or creates overpromise.
- "No risk" or "fully guaranteed."
- Jargon-heavy operational language.

### Visual hierarchy rules

- The section should be easy to scan.
- Steps should feel sequential and reassuring.
- It should support Property Management, not become a separate product.
- CTA should be present but not louder than the main management CTA.

### Language rules

- Process clarity must be equal in ES / EN / FR-CA.
- FR-CA should describe the digital experience and contact path without promising French human communication.

## Global V1 reuse rules

The following can be reused conceptually only if reframed under the V2 hierarchy:

- WhatsApp-first conversion.
- Jacquie/About content.
- Property Management service details.
- Listings as market evidence.
- Pre-construction as a secondary advisory path.
- Miami / Sunny Isles / South Florida focus.
- Confirmed brokerage and Florida real estate / Realtor trust signals, using final formal wording after confirmation.
- Calm premium visual direction and the approved deep plum / aubergine V2 palette.

Reusing an idea does not mean reusing its old placement, priority, or visual weight.

## Definitive V1 eliminations

The Home should definitively remove:

- Portal-style Home structure.
- Catalog-first or search-first logic.
- Equal-priority service hierarchy.
- Listings above trust.
- Pre-construction above trust.
- Project-first navigation.
- Financing tables or investment metrics near the top.
- Generic realtor copy.
- Unverified proof.
- Any section that exists only because V1 had it.

## Copy and message guardrails

Avoid messages that:

- Sell inventory before trust.
- Make Listings or Pre-construction feel like the main product.
- Overpromise returns, income, effortlessness, or guarantees.
- Claim testimonials, years, volume, rankings, awards, or managed-property count without proof.
- Promise French-speaking human communication.
- Use generic luxury language without substance.
- Make Jacquie feel like a platform, portal, or cold service operator.
- Ask the user to browse before they understand why Jacquie can help.

Preferred message direction:

- You have someone trusted in Miami.
- Your property can be cared for and managed with follow-up.
- You can start with a direct conversation.
- Jacquie can help you understand the right path before you commit.

## Global visual hierarchy rules

The Home should visually communicate hierarchy in this order:

1. Trust / Jacquie / direct contact.
2. Property Management.
3. Process.
4. Conversion.
5. Listings as support.
6. Pre-construction as future support, pending brief.

Implementation should avoid:

- Making every section the same size and intensity.
- Giving Listings or Pre-construction the strongest imagery above Property Management.
- Using repeated card grids that flatten priority.
- Making CTA groups look equal when one action is primary.
- Allowing inventory visuals to dominate the first screen.

## ES / EN / FR-CA rules

ES, EN, and FR-CA are first-level MVP languages for the website.

Operational rules:

- Every Home section must exist fully in ES / EN / FR-CA.
- Narrative order must remain consistent across languages.
- CTAs must preserve intent across languages.
- FR-CA must read as a complete site experience, not a partial translation.
- Language route or saved preference should be respected where applicable.
- No language should receive weaker, shorter, placeholder, or missing content.

Communication claim rule:

- Jacquie speaks Spanish and English.
- The website supports ES / EN / FR-CA.
- FR-CA is part of the digital experience and market reach.
- Do not claim direct human communication in French unless Jacquie confirms it.

FR-CA CTA rule:

- Use contact-oriented wording such as "Contacter Jacquie."
- Avoid wording that could imply confirmed French conversation if the surrounding context is ambiguous.

## Implementation risks before Cursor

Before this plan becomes an implementation prompt, these risks must be controlled:

- Cursor may recreate V1's Home order if file names or old components suggest it.
- Existing Listings or Pre-construction components may visually overpower the new hierarchy.
- Property Management may be implemented as a generic service card instead of the main story.
- About/Jacquie may be left too low on the page.
- Old i18n content may treat FR-CA as a partial overlay or secondary translation.
- CTAs may become generic or inconsistent across sections.
- FR-CA CTAs may accidentally imply French-speaking human support.
- Existing project/listing data may pull the Home back into portal behavior.
- Old routes or labels may preserve Esteban-style pre-construction priority.
- Claims may be introduced during copywriting without proof.
- Visual reuse may flatten priority through repeated card grids.
- Search/filter UI may appear too early because it already exists in V1.
- Contact form may compete with WhatsApp if not visually subordinated.
