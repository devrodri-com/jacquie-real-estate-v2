# About Page Brief

## Status

Pre-implementation brief.

No code yet. No Cursor prompt yet.

This document defines the first real version of the `/[locale]/about` page for V2.

## 1. Page Objective

The About page must strengthen personal trust in Jacquie before the user decides to start a conversation.

Its job is to help the user understand:

- who Jacquie is;
- how she accompanies owners, buyers, and investors;
- why having a local person in Miami matters;
- what kind of support they can expect;
- why writing to Jacquie is a reasonable next step.

The page should feel personal, calm, premium, and clear.

It should not become a long biography, a resume, or a generic realtor profile.

## 2. Difference From The Home Trust Section

### Home trust section

The Home trust section introduces Jacquie early.

It should:

- quickly confirm there is a real person behind the site;
- show a few trust signals;
- connect the Hero promise to Property Management;
- keep the user moving through the Home narrative.

### Dedicated About page

The About page should deepen the personal trust layer.

It should:

- explain Jacquie's way of accompanying clients;
- clarify who she helps;
- add a measured human detail, including that she is Argentine;
- show trust signals without inventing proof;
- reinforce personal guidance and direct conversation.

The About page should not repeat the Home section word for word.

## 3. Approved Page Structure

First implementation:

1. Hero About.
2. How I accompany you.
3. Who I work with.
4. Trust signals.
5. My approach.
6. Final CTA.

## 7. CTA Hierarchy

### Primary CTA

Primary CTA should open WhatsApp using the existing helper pattern.

Intent:

> Start a direct conversation with Jacquie.

Labels:

- ES: `Hablar con Jacquie`
- EN: `Talk to Jacquie`
- FR-CA: `Contacter Jacquie`

### Secondary CTA

The About page may include one secondary CTA because users may want to move into the main business service.

Preferred secondary CTA in the Hero:

- ES: `Ver gestión de propiedades`
- EN: `View Property Management`
- FR-CA: `Voir la gestion de propriétés`

Destination:

```text
/{locale}/property-management
```

Final CTA secondary action:

- ES: `Contacto`
- EN: `Contact`
- FR-CA: `Contact`

Destination:

```text
/{locale}/contact
```

Secondary CTAs must be visually quieter than WhatsApp.

## 8. What Not To Include

Do not include:

- Listings;
- Pre-construction;
- property cards;
- project cards;
- search or filters;
- ROI, returns, profitability, occupancy, or investment metrics;
- testimonials unless verified;
- years of experience unless confirmed;
- number of properties managed unless confirmed;
- awards or rankings unless confirmed;
- exact birth city unless confirmed;
- long personal biography;
- resume-like chronology;
- generic luxury realtor copy;
- claims that Jacquie speaks French;
- V1 components, V1 navigation patterns, or V1 catalog logic.

Avoid phrases like:

- `top producer`;
- `award-winning`;
- `best opportunities`;
- `exclusive access`;
- `guaranteed results`;
- `full-service luxury expert`.

## 9. FR-CA Rules

FR-CA is a first-level website experience and must be complete.

Rules:

- Use proper French with accents.
- Public route remains `/fr-ca`.

- Do not expose `/fr`.

Human communication is confirmed in Spanish and English only.

## 10. Acceptance Criteria

The first implementation is acceptable if:

- the page replaces the placeholder with real, complete content;
- ES, EN, and FR-CA have equivalent content quality;
- the page uses first-person voice;
- Jacquie is positioned as a trusted local person in Miami;
- the Argentine detail appears naturally and does not dominate the page;
- WhatsApp is the primary conversion action;
- secondary CTAs are quieter and limited;
- the page expands trust beyond the Home section without repeating it word for word;
- no unconfirmed years, metrics, testimonials, studies, awards, or property counts appear;
- no Listings or Pre-construction appear;
- no direct human French communication is promised;
- the page feels premium, calm, personal, and not corporate;
- it does not copy V1 components or V1 page structure;
- lint and build pass after implementation.
